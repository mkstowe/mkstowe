What is Living Arts?

"The goal of Living Arts is to spur and support collaborative, creative work across disciplinary boundaries and to provide an open, playful environment that encourages students to generate and pursue new ideas."

Learn more about the program here.

Mentoring

During my second year of college, I was a peer-mentor to a group of three first-year students. As a mentor, I led my group - as well as other new students in the community - through various projects and events. The goal was to help transition into college, help these students learn more about their creative processes, and be a source of information and advice. Two of the students in my group even went on to become mentors to their own groups for the next wave of students!

Workshops

To encourage creativity in different disciplines, the community participated in various workshops which were often led by professionals in the respective fields.

Movie

For the first workshop of the year, we got into groups to tell a story through a five minute movie. We learned about the process of creating a story, filming, and editing the video. We were able to witness first-hand the creative process involved in both the technical and the imaginative parts of film making.

Dance

The goal of this workshop was for the community to get to know each other and to express our creativity through movement. Led by Professor of Dance Amy Chavasse, we went undertook different activities that utilized the body's senses to embrace an unorthodox method of creative outlet.

Video Game

Lecturer Austin Yarger guided the community through a workshop that explored the creativity involved with video games. We learned about parts of the process of making video games, and we even came up with ideas for our own games. Finally, we learned about how coding, art, music, and other disciplines are all involved in making a video game - using Toby Fox's Undertale as an example.

Culture

For the last of the workshops, the Living Arts Residential Advisers led a workshop that involved the groups creating their own society and culture based on different snacks. We got to have fun by going back to elementary school days and using tri-boards to make presentations about the creative societies that we came up with.

Creative Collaborative Project (CCP)

The grand finale of each year in Living Arts is the Creative Collaborate Project. This is where each mentor/mentee group works together to create a project that encapsulates some topic. The groups can be as concise or as broad as they like. Once all of the projects were complete. the entire community joins to share our projects and to experience what all of our peers came up with.

Typewriter

My group was assigned the word "Evolve" as our topic for the CCP. We decided that rather than make a project that explains what it means to evolve, we would make a project that was a form of evolution itself. To do this, we bought an antique typewriter and placed it in various locations around campus. Each location was a new "chapter" and started with a quote from a literary work. With the typewriter set up with the prompt, we just left it for passersby to interact with it however they saw fit. This resulted in a continually evolving story full of emotion, hilarity, and nonsense.
